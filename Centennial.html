Ball State University is celebrating its centennial year. One hundred years ago, the five Ball Brothers purchased a defunct institution and donated it to the state of Indiana. What was once called Indiana State Normal School Eastern Division was renamed Ball State University in honor of the generosity of the Ball Brothers. In its inaugural year, Ball State was solely a college for teachers. Now, it has seven colleges that serve nearly 22,000 students a year. In the past 100 years, Ball State has accumulated a lot of history and the students have created many traditions. The university is officially celebrating the centennial Sept. 6 and in the days leading up, The Daily News will count down by sharing the history of a person, place or tradition that Cardinals past and present have come to know.
